About

Modern AI progress is increasingly driven not only by model architecture and compute, but also by the scale, quality, diversity, curation, governance, and transformation of data. Recent breakthroughs in large language models, multimodal foundation models, diffusion models, embodied agents, scientific AI, and autonomous systems all depend on complex data pipelines spanning collection, filtering, annotation, synthesis, compression, alignment, evaluation, and continual refinement. Yet the research community still lacks a unified forum for systematically studying the data revolution behind modern AI, what data is needed, how it should be constructed, how it shapes model behavior, and how it can be made more efficient, trustworthy, and socially beneficial.

This workshop brings together researchers and practitioners working on data-centric AI, foundation model training, dataset construction, synthetic data, data quality evaluation, data attribution, data governance, and AI safety. Instead of treating data as a static input to model training, the workshop emphasizes data as an active research object, a resource to be designed, optimized, audited, compressed, synthesized, protected, and governed. We organize topics along the data lifecycle (construct → optimize → evaluate → ensure trust), together with the modalities, domains, and temporal dynamics that cut across every stage.

Call For Papers

Workshop Scope

We welcome submissions and discussions on topics along the data lifecycle (construct → optimize → evaluate → ensure trust), together with the modalities, domains, and temporal dynamics that cut across every stage:

🏗️

Theme 1: Data Construction and Synthesis

  • Data collection filtering, deduplication, and curation for foundation models
  • Synthetic data generation, verification, and evaluation
  • Instruction tuning preference data, human feedback, and alignment datasets
  • Human–AI collaborative data creation, annotation, and revision workflows
⚡

Theme 2: Data Optimization and Efficiency

  • Data mixture optimization and scaling laws for pretraining, post-training, and domain adaptation
  • Dataset distillation data compression, data pruning, and compute-efficient data selection
🔬

Theme 3: Data Evaluation, Attribution, and Quality

  • Data attribution valuation, influence estimation, and memorization analysis
  • Data contamination benchmark leakage, and evaluation reliability
  • Quality metrics diversity measures, coverage analysis, and distribution-shift detection
🛡️

Theme 4: Data Behavior, Safety, and Governance

  • Hallucination reduction factuality, robustness, and safety
  • Privacy & copyright licensing, governance, and responsible use of large-scale datasets
🌐

Theme 5: Modalities, Domains, and Temporal Dynamics

  • Multimodal datasets for language, vision, audio, video, robotics, agents, and scientific AI
  • Continual data updating, dynamic benchmarks, and lifelong data management
  • Domain-specific data pipelines for healthcare, science, education, agriculture, finance, climate, and other high-impact applications
  • Empirical studies theoretical analyses, benchmarks, systems, tools, and best practices for data-driven AI development
